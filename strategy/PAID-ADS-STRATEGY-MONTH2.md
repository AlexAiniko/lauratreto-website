## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Month 1 (April) is organic-only. Month 2 (May) introduces paid ads to amplify what is already working organically. The strategy is Meta-first (Instagram + Facebook), with TikTok ads added in Week 3 once Meta campaigns are stable. Total monthly budget: $750-$1,000. Primary funnel: ad to quiz to email to booking.

May is a favorable month for fitness ads. Industry CPL data shows April-May has the lowest cost per lead of the year (as low as $13.50 vs. the $29.70 annual average), dipping 64% below global averages. This is the best time to start.

## 1. MONTHLY AD BUDGET

Laura is bootstrapping on ~$600/mo revenue. The ad budget must be aggressive enough to generate leads but not reckless.

| Item | Monthly Budget |
|------|---------------|

| TikTok Ads (Weeks 3-4 only) | $200-$300 |

**Daily breakdown:**
- Meta: $17-$22/day (above the $20/day ad group minimum)

**Why this works:** At a $15-$20 CPL on Meta (realistic for fitness in May), $600 in Meta spend generates 30-40 leads. If 10% convert to a Movement Assessment ($125), that is 3-4 bookings = $375-$500 in direct revenue. The quiz also builds the email list for ongoing nurture.

**Funding note:** If April organic efforts bring in even 1-2 new clients, that revenue ($250-$500) can fund the entire May ad budget. Zero-risk if timed right.

## 2. PLATFORM ALLOCATION

### Meta (Instagram + Facebook): 65-70% of budget

**Why Meta first:**
- Laura has warm audiences on both platforms (1,700 IG + 1,900 FB followers)
- Retargeting website visitors and quiz takers is Meta's strength
- Instagram Reels and Stories are ideal for fitness creative
- Meta's ad platform is more mature, easier to optimize at small budgets
- Lower minimum spend than TikTok ($1/day vs. $20/day at ad group level)

### TikTok: 30-35% of budget (Weeks 3-4 only)

**Why TikTok second:**
- Laura has the largest audience here (13.9K followers)
- Health & Fitness CPM is the lowest category on TikTok ($6.50)
- BUT: TikTok's minimum campaign budget is $500, and daily ad group minimum is $20
- Better to let Meta campaigns stabilize first, then layer TikTok in

### Google Ads: Not yet

Google is expensive for fitness ($5.26 avg CPC vs. $1.92 on Meta). Save for Month 4+ when there is budget headroom and testimonial/review content to fuel search ads.

## 3. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Meta Ads: 3-Campaign Funnel

**Campaign 1: AWARENESS (Video Views)**
- Objective: Video Views
- Budget: $5/day ($150/mo)
- Purpose: Build warm audiences of people who watch 50%+ of Laura's videos
- Creative: Top-performing organic Reels from April (repurposed)
- Audience: Cold, local Key West (15-mile radius) + broad interest-based (women 30-60, fitness, wellness, dance)
- Why: Creates a retargeting pool cheaply. Video views cost $0.01-$0.03 each.

**Campaign 2: LEAD GENERATION (Quiz Funnel)**
- Objective: Conversions (optimize for quiz completions)
- Budget: $10-$12/day ($300-$360/mo)
- Purpose: Drive quiz completions and email capture
- Creative: Direct-response video ads (new, scripted for ads)
- Landing page: lauratreto.com/quiz
- Audience: Warm (video viewers from Campaign 1) + Lookalike (1% of quiz completers) + Interest-based cold
- Why: This is the money campaign. Every quiz completion = an email + segmentation data.

**Campaign 3: RETARGETING (Booking)**
- Objective: Conversions (optimize for assessment bookings)
- Budget: $3-$5/day ($90-$150/mo)
- Purpose: Convert quiz takers and website visitors to booked assessments
- Creative: Testimonials, "what to expect" video, limited-slot urgency
- Audience: Quiz completers who did not book (7-day and 14-day windows), website visitors (30 days)
- Why: Cheapest conversions come from people who already know you. Retargeting CPAs are typically 50-70% lower.

### TikTok Ads: 1 Campaign (Weeks 3-4)

**Campaign: LEAD GENERATION**
- Objective: Lead Generation (TikTok native lead form OR traffic to quiz)
- Budget: $15-$20/day
- Creative: Top-performing TikTok organic videos + 1-2 new UGC-style ads
- Audience: Key West + Florida + Women 25-55 interested in fitness, wellness, yoga, dance
- Format: In-Feed ads (NOT TopView or branded effects, too expensive)
- Why: Test TikTok's lead gen at minimum viable budget. If CPL is under $20, scale in Month 3.

## 4. AD FORMATS & CREATIVE STRATEGY

### What works for fitness coaches in 2026

Ranked by performance (best first):

1. **Talking-head video (15-30 sec):** Laura speaking directly to camera about a specific problem. Outperforms generic montages by 3x. Example: "If you're over 40 and your back hurts every morning, here's what's actually going on."

2. **Before/after coaching moment (15-30 sec):** Real client correction or movement improvement. Raw footage, no heavy editing. "Watch me fix this in 30 seconds."

3. **Testimonial video (15-30 sec):** Client (Carol, Emma, Tony) sharing results in their own words. UGC-style, filmed on phone.

4. **Carousel (Instagram only):** Education-based. "5 Signs Your Body Is Compensating" with swipeable slides. Good for retargeting.

5. **Stories/Reels placement:** Same video creative, auto-placed in Stories and Reels. Vertical 9:16 only.

### Creative rules

- All video must be vertical (9:16)
- First 3 seconds = pattern interrupt (movement, bold text, direct question)
- Captions/subtitles on every video (80%+ of social video is watched muted)
- No stock footage, no generic gym B-roll
- Show Laura's face in the first frame (builds trust, stops scroll)
- Bilingual: Run separate ad sets for English and Spanish creative (do NOT mix in one ad)

### Month 2 creative production plan

| Asset | Type | Quantity | Source |
|-------|------|----------|--------|
| Repurposed organic winners | Video | 3-5 | Best-performing April Reels |
| Direct-response quiz ads | Video | 3 new | Scripted, filmed at batch session |
| Testimonial clips | Video | 2-3 | Filmed with Carol, Emma |
| Retargeting "what to expect" | Video | 1 | Studio/outdoor, walkthrough of assessment |
| Carousel education posts | Static | 2-3 | Canva, based on quiz topics |

**Total new creative needed: 6-9 assets for the entire month.**

Film everything in one Saturday batch session (already scheduled weekly).

### Broader Online (for Strong Lean Athletic program + virtual coaching)

- **Week 1-2:** Interest-based cold targeting + warm retargeting of April organic audience

- Location: Florida (Key West geo is too small for TikTok's algorithm)
- Age: 25-55
- Interest: Fitness & gym, yoga, dance, wellness
- Let TikTok's algorithm optimize (it works best with broad targeting and strong creative)

## 6. THE FUNNEL: AD TO QUIZ TO EMAIL TO BOOKING

```
[Ad] --> [Quiz Landing Page] --> [Quiz Completion + Email Capture] --> [Results Page + CTA]
                                          |                                    |
                                          v                                    v
                                   [Email Sequence]                    [Book Assessment]
                                   (5-email nurture)                   (direct conversion)
                                          |
                                          v
                                   [Retargeting Ads]
                                   (didn't book yet)
```

### Step-by-step

1. **Ad (Meta/TikTok):** Hook with a movement problem or question. CTA: "Take the free quiz."
2. **Quiz (lauratreto.com/quiz):** Movement Readiness Score. 8-10 questions. Takes 2 minutes. Captures email at end.
3. **Results page:** Personalized score + recommendation. CTA: "Book your Movement Assessment ($125)" or "Join Strong Lean Athletic ($149)."
4. **Email sequence (ConvertKit):** 5 emails over 10 days. Value-first, not salesy.
   - Email 1 (immediate): Your results + 1 free tip based on score
   - Email 2 (Day 2): Laura's story (dancer to coach, psychology background)
   - Email 3 (Day 4): "What I see that other trainers miss" (authority content)
   - Email 4 (Day 7): Client testimonial + social proof
   - Email 5 (Day 10): Direct offer with urgency ("3 assessment slots open this week")
5. **Retargeting ads:** Hit quiz completers who did not book with testimonial and urgency creative.

### Why quiz funnels outperform standard lead magnets

- Quiz funnels convert 30-50% of visitors vs. 3-10% for PDF lead magnets
- Zero-party data from quiz responses enables personalized follow-up
- The quiz itself is the value (people want their score), so it feels like a service, not a transaction
- Laura's quiz is already built and live. This is a massive head start.

## 8. TESTING FRAMEWORK

### Week 1-2: Test creative (most important variable)

Run 3 ad creatives per ad set. Keep audience and placement identical. After 72 hours and 1,000+ impressions per creative, kill the lowest performer. Winning creative gets 70% of budget.

**What to test:**
- Hook style: question vs. bold claim vs. visual pattern interrupt
- Laura talking to camera vs. coaching a client vs. movement demo
- English vs. Spanish (separate ad sets)

### Week 2-3: Test audiences

Once winning creative is identified, duplicate it into 3 audience ad sets:
- Interest-based cold
- Warm retarget (video viewers + website visitors)
- Lookalike (if enough quiz data)

### Week 3-4: Test offers

- "Take the free quiz" vs. "Book a free 15-min call" vs. "Get your movement score"
- Direct assessment booking vs. quiz-first funnel

### Testing rules

- Change ONE variable at a time
- Minimum 72 hours and $30-$50 per test before making decisions
- Do not turn off ads based on 1 day of data
- Document every test and result in a shared spreadsheet

## 11. WHAT NOT TO DO

1. **Do not boost posts.** Boosting is Meta's way of taking your money with no targeting control. Use Ads Manager only.
2. **Do not run ads before the pixel has 48 hours of data.** Install pixel in Week 1 of April if possible.
3. **Do not change budgets by more than 20% at a time.** Large budget swings reset Meta's learning phase.
4. **Do not target too narrow on TikTok.** TikTok's algorithm needs broad audiences to optimize.
5. **Do not run ads to a slow website.** Quiz page must load in under 3 seconds on mobile. Test this.
6. **Do not skip the email sequence.** Ads without email follow-up waste 80% of leads.
7. **Do not expect Month 2 to be profitable.** Month 2 is data collection. Profitability comes Month 3-4 when you have audiences, creative winners, and email list momentum.
8. **Do not run only one creative.** Always test 2-3 creatives minimum. Single-creative campaigns die fast.
