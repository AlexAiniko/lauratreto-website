### Pillar 1: Movement Authority (40%)

The core. Laura sees things in human movement that personal trainers, PTs, and gym coaches miss -- because she spent a decade performing at the highest level of physical art on earth.

**Specific Content Ideas:**

1. **"The #1 Mistake I See in Every Gym"** -- Film yourself correcting someone's squat (with permission). Show the wrong pattern, then the correction. Hook: "I've watched 1,000 professional dancers move. This squat mistake would get you pulled from a show." CTA: DM MOVE.

2. **"Your Hips Are Lying to You"** -- Demonstrate the difference between hip flexor tightness and anterior pelvic tilt. Most people stretch the wrong thing. Show the test, then the fix. Hook: "You don't have tight hip flexors. You have lazy glutes."

3. **"Why Dancers Never Get Knee Injuries (And Gym People Do)"** -- Explain knee tracking, turnout mechanics, and how dance training teaches joint integrity that fitness culture ignores. Voiceover with demo.

4. **"Stop Stretching Your Hamstrings"** -- Counterintuitive take. Most hamstring "tightness" is neural tension or pelvic positioning. Show the test. Show what to do instead. Hook: "Your hamstrings aren't tight. Your brain thinks they are."

5. **"The Balance Test That Predicts Falls After 60"** -- Single-leg stance with eyes closed. Film yourself doing it, then coach the viewer through it. Hook: "If you can't do this for 10 seconds, your fall risk just doubled."

6. **"Watch Me Fix This Deadlift in 30 Seconds"** -- Real coaching moment (with client consent). Show the before, the cue, the after. No fancy editing needed. Raw is better. Hook: "One sentence changed this entire lift."

7. **"Your Shoulder Isn't the Problem"** -- Demonstrate how thoracic spine mobility drives overhead movement. Most shoulder pain is a thoracic mobility deficit. Show a wall angel test + correction drill.

8. **"3 Exercises Every Desk Worker Needs (That Aren't Stretching)"** -- Controlled articular rotations for neck, thoracic, and hips. Quick demo format. Text overlay. No talking needed.

9. **"How a Dancer Warms Up vs. How a Gym Person Warms Up"** -- Side-by-side comparison. Dance warm-up is full-body, neurological, articular. Gym warm-up is 5 min on the treadmill. Show the difference in prep quality.

10. **"The Ankle Mobility Drill Nobody Does"** -- Banded ankle dorsiflexion or wall drill. Hook: "If your ankles don't move, nothing above them works right. Knees, hips, back -- all compensating."

11. **"Walking Wrong Is Destroying Your Back"** -- Gait analysis. Show heel strike, arm swing, trunk rotation. Most people walk like they're being dragged. Film at a busy KW street and narrate (no faces).

12. **"Why I Don't Count Reps"** -- Explain quality-over-quantity philosophy from dance training. In rehearsal, you don't do 3 sets of 12. You do it until it's right. Apply to fitness.

13. **"The Posture Fix That Takes 10 Seconds"** -- Rib cage positioning over pelvis. Not "shoulders back" -- that's the wrong cue. Show the real alignment fix.

### Pillar 2: Trauma / Empowerment (25%)

Laura's clinical psychology degree and lived experience make this pillar authentic. This is NOT therapy content. This is the intersection of body and emotion -- how movement heals, how pain stores in the body, how confidence returns through physical competence.

**Specific Content Ideas:**

5. **"The Exercise That Made My Client Cry (In a Good Way)"** -- Story format. A client who couldn't get off the floor without help, and the day they stood up unassisted. What that meant for their independence and dignity.

6. **"Stop Punishing Your Body"** -- Address exercise-as-punishment culture. Reframe movement as a conversation with your body, not a war against it. Especially for women 35-50 recovering from diet culture.

7. **"What Happens When You Move Without Pain for the First Time in Years"** -- Describe the emotional response Laura sees in clients. The surprise, the tears, the "I forgot I could feel like this." Powerful testimonial-style content.

8. **"Your Doctor Said 'Just Exercise More' -- Now What?"** -- Bridge the gap between medical advice and execution. Most people don't need motivation. They need someone who understands their fear, their limitations, and their starting point.

9. **"From Havana to Key West: How Dance Saved My Life"** -- Personal origin story. Cultural background, why she dances, what leaving Cuba meant, how movement became her constant. Deep connection content. Film once, repurpose many times.

10. **"The Confidence Loop: Move Better, Feel Better, Move More"** -- Explain the positive feedback loop between physical competence and self-esteem. When you can DO things with your body, you trust yourself more everywhere.

11. **"Why I Never Say 'No Pain No Gain'"** -- Dismantle the toxic fitness mantra. Pain is information. In dance, pain means something is wrong and you fix it before it ends your career. Apply to everyday fitness.

12. **"Breathing Is the Most Underrated Exercise"** -- Diaphragmatic breathing demo. Explain the vagus nerve connection, stress response, and why 90% of her new clients can't take a proper breath.

### Template 1: Pattern Interrupt Hook

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Format** | Talking head to camera |
| **Length** | 45-60 seconds |
| **Best for** | Movement Authority, Trauma/Empowerment |
| **Platforms** | TikTok first, then IG Reels |

**Structure:**
```
[0-3 sec]  HOOK: Controversial or surprising statement. Say it like you mean it.
[3-35 sec] BODY: "Here's what I mean..." Explain with authority. One clear point.
[35-45 sec] PROOF: Quick credential or experience reference.
[45-60 sec] CTA: One clear action.
```

**Example (film this today):**

> **HOOK:** "I don't care if you can touch your toes."
>
> **BODY:** "Flexibility without control is just instability with extra steps. What I care about is whether you can CONTROL the range you have. That's mobility. That's what keeps you moving at 60, 70, 80. I coached bodies through over a thousand performances with Acosta Danza -- and the flexible ones who couldn't control it? They got injured the most."
>
> **CTA:** "If you're in Key West and want someone who actually knows the difference -- DM me ASSESSMENT."

**When to use:** Any time Laura has a strong opinion or counterintuitive take. This is her signature format.

## 3. FORMAT LIBRARY

| # | Format | Description | Length | Production Time | Platform Priority | Best Pillar |

| 10 | **AI Quote / Infographic** | Canva or AI-generated graphic | N/A (static) | 5 min | IG Feed, FB | Trauma, Movement |

### Format Details

**1. Talking Head**
- Setup: Phone on tripod, vertical, natural light preferred, DJI mic
- Background: clean wall, outdoor KW location, or home setup
- Laura looks directly at camera, speaks clearly, uses hands
- Always add captions (CapCut auto-caption or manual)
- This is Laura's strongest format -- lean into it

**2. Movement Demo**
- Setup: Tripod at 45-degree angle or straight-on, full body visible
- Wear form-fitting clothes so movement is visible
- One exercise per video. Clear start and end.
- Text overlay with exercise name and rep/set prescription
- No music needed -- ambient sound or voiceover

**3. Split Screen**
- Use CapCut split-screen template
- Left side: wrong pattern. Right side: correct pattern.
- Laura demos both. Or: before/after client footage.
- Text labels: "THIS" (with X) vs. "THIS" (with checkmark)
- Film both angles in the same session

**4. Text Overlay on Movement**
- Film a beautiful movement clip (dance flow, stretching, training)
- Add 3-5 bold text cards that tell a story or teach a lesson
- Each text card on screen for 3-4 seconds
- Works great for B-roll repurposing

**5. Carousel**
- Slide 1: Bold hook ("5 Signs Your Hips Are Lying to You")
- Slides 2-6: One point per slide, minimal text, clean design
- Slide 7: CTA ("Save this. Share it. DM me MOVE.")
- Use Canva templates or AI image generation
- High save rate = algorithm boost on IG

**6. Lifestyle / BTS Montage**
- Collect 5-8 short clips throughout the day (phone camera)
- Stitch in CapCut with transitions and music
- Text overlays for context ("6 AM dock mobility," "sunset walk")
- Keep it aspirational but real -- not polished, not sloppy

**7. Livestream**
- Go live from interesting locations: pool, beach, waterfront, gym
- Have 3 talking points prepared but let it flow naturally
- Engage with comments in real time
- FB Live reaches the 50+ audience better than any other format
- Save and repurpose clips as short-form content

**8. Client Story**
- Get client consent on camera or in writing
- Film client speaking about their experience (30 sec)
- OR Laura narrates over B-roll of training session
- Specific results: "went from X to Y in Z weeks"
- End with CTA

**9. Couple Content**
- Keep it natural -- don't over-produce
- Best when it's genuinely funny or sweet, not staged
- The contrast between Laura's pro career and Alex gamely learning is the goldmine (Alex dances well -- play the contrast, not incompetence)
- Tag each other for cross-audience exposure

**10. AI Quote / Infographic**
- Use for low-effort days or to fill the feed
- Quote Laura's own words from other videos
- Clean design, brand colors (turquoise, coral, white)
- Never more than 2 per week -- the feed needs real faces
