## How to Use This Document

The primary action plan assumes best-case execution. This playbook activates when something breaks. Each scenario has:

- **Trigger:** The specific signal that tells you you're in this scenario.
- **Decision deadline:** The date by which you must act, not just observe.
- **Ranked actions:** Do #1 first. Move to #2 only if #1 doesn't move the needle.
- **Expected outcome:** What "working" looks like.

Do not activate a contingency prematurely. Give the primary plan its full runway. But when the trigger fires, act on the same day.

## Scenario 1: Zero Local Bookings After 2 Weeks

### What failure looks like by April 21

Zero movement assessments booked from local Key West contacts. No DM conversations converting to a scheduled call. Carol Whiteman remains the only in-person client. The networking events (Sweat Society, Studios of Key West) produced warm conversations but no paying action.

Note: this is not catastrophic at Week 2. The revenue model projects Month 1 local revenue at $500-$1,000 including the baseline. But if nothing is moving by April 21, the pipeline is broken somewhere and must be diagnosed before Week 3.

### Trigger

Zero local assessment bookings as of end of day Monday, April 21.

### Decision deadline

Wednesday, April 23. By this date, a pivot action must be underway, not planned.

### Diagnostic questions (answer these before acting)

1. Awareness: Has anyone outside of Carol actually heard of Laura in Key West? Did the Facebook group posts land? Did Sweat Society conversations happen? If no one knows she exists, the problem is reach, not conversion.

2. Pricing: Did anyone express interest and then go quiet after hearing $125? If yes, the problem may be price anchoring without enough perceived value established first.

3. Trust: Are people warm but hesitant? "I'll think about it" responses indicate the credential story is not landing with enough force in conversation or content.

4. Wrong audience: Is the Sweat Society crowd 25-35-year-old athletes who feel fine? The target is adults 45+ who feel stiff, sore, or disconnected. If the networking venues skew young and athletic, the message is hitting the wrong room.

### Ranked actions

**Action 1: Host a free 45-minute outdoor group class at Truman Waterfront Park (target date: April 24-26)**

This is the fastest way to put Laura in front of 10-20 local adults with no barrier to entry. Format: mobility and movement flow, outdoor, no equipment needed. Promote in every Key West Facebook group starting April 21. Post a TikTok and IG reel announcing it. Send personal DMs to every warm contact from the first two weeks. Goal: 10 people show up. Goal from those 10: 2-3 book a paid assessment. The outdoor setting, Laura's credentials, and a live demonstration of her coaching style will convert better than any DM template.

Logistics: Truman Waterfront Park is free and public. No permit required for a small group. Laura already has all equipment (iPhone, tripod, DJI mic). Film the session for content. One event creates 5-10 pieces of content.

**Action 2: Activate a referral incentive for Carol Whiteman**

Carol is a paying client who presumably has a social circle in Key West. Offer her one free session in exchange for introducing Laura to one person who books a paid assessment. This is not a discount on Laura's services. Carol gets a bonus session. The referred contact pays full price. Script for Laura: "Carol, you've been such a great client. I'm building my Key West practice and I'd love to meet more people like you. If you know anyone who has been dealing with stiffness, old injuries, or just feeling off in their body, I'd love to meet them. If they book a session, I'll add a free session to your package as a thank-you."

**Action 3: Approach one Key West business for a guest workshop**

Target: a yoga studio, a pickleball club, or a chiropractic office whose clients are in Laura's target demographic (45+, active, dealing with physical limitations). Offer a free 30-minute workshop for their members on "movement longevity." No charge to the business. Laura gives the workshop, collects emails at the door with a sign-up sheet, and pitches the Movement Readiness assessment at the close. One workshop in front of 15 targeted adults is worth 10 weeks of generic social posts.

Best targets: Body Zone Key West, any pickleball or tennis club, any chiropractic or physical therapy clinic (PT-bridge niche is natural here).

**Action 4: Drop the assessment price to $97 for a 48-hour "Key West Launch Offer"**

This is the last resort. Alpha's strong recommendation is to hold the $125 price through at least Month 2. A discount this early signals insecurity, not generosity. However, if actions 1-3 produce zero bookings by April 28, a time-limited $97 introductory rate for "the first 5 Key West clients" is acceptable. Frame it as exclusivity, not desperation. "I'm opening 5 spots for Key West residents at an introductory rate before my schedule fills up." This framing is honest and holds the premium positioning. After 5 clients, price returns to $125 permanently.

### Expected outcome if pivot works

2-4 local assessments booked by April 28. At least 1 converts to a 4-pack or 8-pack. Laura has real local momentum entering Week 4, which is when hotel outreach and the Salsa Sunset event run.

## Scenario 2: Content Volume Drops (Perfectionism Wins)

### What failure looks like

Laura posts 2-3 times in Week 1 instead of the daily target. The reason given is that the content "isn't ready" or "doesn't look right." By April 14 (start of Week 2), the TikTok Live rhythm is not established and the feed has fewer than 7 posts.

### Trigger

Fewer than 5 feed posts across all platforms by end of day Sunday, April 13.

### Decision deadline

Monday, April 14, first thing in the morning. The Week 2 calendar cannot start with Week 1's deficit unaddressed.

### How to reset without shame or pressure

The conversation is not "you didn't post enough." The conversation is "the first posts are the hardest and you got through them. Now we simplify the format so the friction is lower." No lecture. No review of what should have happened. Immediate pivot to a lower-resistance format that still ships content.

Alex's role: a daily check-in text, not a performance review. One message at 9 PM: "Did a post go out today?" Yes or no. If no, the next message is: "What's one thing that happened today with movement, a client, or your body? Film a 60-second talking head right now. Don't edit it. Post it."

### Emergency content formats (zero editing required)

These formats require one take, no editing, no captions added manually, no Canva, no CapCut. They post in under 10 minutes from pickup to publish.

**Format A: The 60-second morning thought**
Laura picks up her phone in the morning, before she is "ready." Messy hair is fine. Outside is fine. She speaks directly to camera for 60 seconds about one movement observation, a client moment, or something she noticed in her own body. No script. No hook. Just her voice. Post with auto-captions enabled on TikTok. Caption: 1 sentence + 5 hashtags.

Why it works: Instagram analytics showed that personal vulnerability outperforms polished educational content. Laura's raw, real voice is a content asset, not a liability.

**Format B: The client session BTS clip**
During Carol's session (Mon/Wed/Fri 9 AM), Laura props her phone against something and films 30 seconds of the session. No editing. Post the clip with a one-line text overlay in TikTok ("What we drilled today" or the name of the exercise). Done in 5 minutes after the session.

**Format C: The reaction video**
Laura finds a fitness or movement claim on TikTok's For You page that she disagrees with or wants to add to. She records herself reacting, using TikTok's built-in duet or stitch function. No filming setup needed. The reaction is the content. This format often outperforms produced content because TikTok's algorithm promotes stitch and duet engagement.

**Format D: The question post**
Laura posts a single question to her IG Story or TikTok: "What part of your body feels the worst when you wake up in the morning?" Poll feature enabled. This generates comments and engagement without requiring any performance from Laura. Respond to every comment with a specific answer. That engagement becomes content.

### Accountability structure

Daily: Alex texts "Post today?" at 9 PM.
Weekly: Friday KPI check includes post count. Target is 7 posts per week (1 per day minimum). If count is below 5, the following week uses only emergency formats. No produced content until the volume habit is established.

The goal in Week 1 is not quality. The goal is 7 posts. Quality comes after the posting muscle is built.

The Tuesday and Thursday morning sessions are primary social touchpoints for Week 1 and Week 2. If Sweat Society cancels events, changes schedule, or turns out to be the wrong demographic (young competitive athletes rather than 45+ adults with movement needs), the community-building strategy needs a backup.

### Trigger

Either (a) Sweat Society cancels or is unavailable for 2+ scheduled sessions, or (b) Laura attends 3 sessions and has zero warm conversations that fit the target client profile.

### Decision deadline

End of Week 2, April 19.

### Ranked actions

**Action 1: Activate the solo pop-up class at Higgs Beach or Truman Waterfront**

This does not require Sweat Society and is entirely within Laura's control. Higgs Beach is one of Key West's most trafficked outdoor recreation spots. Truman Waterfront Park is large, open, and free. A Tuesday or Thursday morning outdoor class at 8:30 AM positions Laura exactly where active adults 45+ already are. No permit required for small informal groups. Promote via Facebook groups 3 days in advance. Film the session for content. Even if only 3 people show up, Laura gets real people, real coaching footage, and a repeatable weekly event she owns and controls.

This is also a better content asset than gym footage. Outdoor, ocean-adjacent, Laura coaching real people in Key West, natural light. Every pop-up class generates a week's worth of content.

**Action 2: Reach out directly to Studios of Key West**

The Tuesday 6 PM networking event at Studios of Key West is already on the Week 1 calendar (April 8). If Sweat Society is not producing results, double down on Studios of Key West and request a formal introduction slot or guest speaker opportunity. Studios of Key West is a mixed-use creative and wellness space that serves exactly Laura's target demographic. A 5-minute "movement tip" presentation at one of their events would be worth 10 Sweat Society sessions.

**Action 3: Join and activate in Key West Facebook groups**

The primary action calendar calls for joining 3 groups in Week 1 but only observing initially. If in-person networking underperforms, accelerate to active participation in these groups by Week 2. Post a proper introduction with credentials. Offer a free 20-minute virtual movement assessment to the first 3 people who respond. This is a lead generation tactic, not a service giveaway. Key West Facebook groups to target: KW Community Bulletin Board, What's Happening in Key West, Key West Locals, any pickleball or tennis Facebook groups if they exist.

**Action 4: Approach 1-2 hotel wellness coordinators**

Week 4 (April 28 - May 4) already has hotel outreach planned. If community building stalls in Weeks 1-2, move one hotel outreach conversation earlier. Key West hotels with wellness amenities (Pier House, Casa Marina, Hyatt Centric, The Gardens Hotel) have guests who are exactly Laura's target: adults 45+, high income, on vacation and open to trying something new. A hotel partnership where Laura offers paid private sessions to hotel guests is a reliable revenue channel with no social media required.

### Expected outcome if pivot works

By end of Week 2, Laura has run at least one outdoor pop-up class with 5+ attendees, has warm conversations with 3-5 potential local clients, and has one ongoing relationship with a local business, event space, or hotel.

## Scenario 4: Paid Ads Underperform (Month 2)

### Context

Paid Meta ads are scheduled to begin in Month 2 (May 2026) after 3 months of organic content has been established. Alpha's recommendation is to hold off on ads until a content library exists and the funnel is proven. However, the contingency plan below applies if ads launch and underperform.

### What "underperforming" looks like

CPL (cost per lead, defined as a quiz completion or a DM initiated from an ad) exceeds $30 on Meta. Creative fatigue sets in: CTR drops below 1% within the first 2 weeks. The ads are generating clicks but no conversions past the first touchpoint.

### Trigger

After 14 days of active Meta ads, CPL is above $30 OR total ad spend has exceeded $300 with fewer than 10 qualified leads generated.

### Decision deadline

At the 14-day mark. Do not let a failing campaign run for 30 days "to gather more data." At $300 spent with weak results, the creative or targeting is wrong.

### Ranked actions

**Action 1: Pause and audit before touching budget**

Stop the campaign. Do not increase budget. Do not change targeting. First, audit the creative and landing experience:

- Is the hook in the first 3 seconds of the ad video stopping the scroll? If CTR is below 1%, the hook is the problem.
- Is the landing page (website or quiz) loading fast on mobile? A slow quiz kills conversions regardless of creative quality.
- Is the audience targeting too broad? For Key West local ads, a 25-mile radius with age 40-65 and interests in wellness, fitness, or dance is the starting point. For digital (SLA), women 35-45 with interests in body recomposition and online coaching.

Fix one variable at a time. The most common failure mode is changing too many things at once and losing the signal.

**Action 2: Test Spanish-language creative against English**

The State of Business Report identifies Spanish-language ads as a significant advantage: 60-90% cheaper CPMs than English, no dominant competitor in premium movement coaching in Spanish. If English ads underperform, launch one Spanish-language creative with the same targeting. The comparison data will reveal whether language is the leverage point.

Laura can record a 30-second Spanish-language ad in one take using the emergency content format from Scenario 2. No production required. Authentic Spanish-language coaching content from a Cuban movement coach is inherently distinctive in the feed.

**Action 3: Shift budget to retargeting only**

If cold-audience ads are underperforming, redirect the remaining budget to retargeting: people who visited the website, watched at least 50% of a video, or engaged with an IG or Facebook post. Retargeting audiences are warmer, convert at higher rates, and cost less per conversion. The organic content from Weeks 1-6 will have built a retargetable audience of several hundred to a few thousand people. That audience should convert at CPL well below $30.

**Action 4: Organic-only Month 2 fallback**

If CPL remains above $30 after testing Spanish creative and retargeting, pause all paid ads and shift to organic for the remainder of Month 2. This is not a failure. The revenue model does not require paid ads to hit Month 2 targets ($1,800-$3,100). The ads were acceleration, not foundation. Extend the organic-only period through Month 3 and relaunch ads in Month 4 with 90+ days of content, a proven quiz funnel, and SLA testimonials from beta buyers.

The Spanish-language ad advantage remains intact for Month 4 relaunch. A competitor who hasn't executed bilingual content cannot catch up quickly.

### Expected outcome if pivot works

CPL drops to $15-20 on retargeting audiences. Spanish-language creative opens a lower-cost acquisition channel. Ad spend is preserved for Month 4 when the organic foundation is stronger and the funnel is proven.

## Scenario 5: Strong Lean Athletic (SLA) Doesn't Launch on Time

### Context

The Strong Lean Athletic 12-week digital program is filmed and edited but needs final packaging. Target for soft launch is Week 2-3 (April 14-21). The program has never been sold, has no testimonials, and the email list to sell it to is being built from scratch via the Movement Readiness quiz.

### What failure looks like

By April 21 (end of Week 2), SLA is still not ready to sell because packaging is incomplete, Trainerize checkout is not set up, or the program content itself needs additional work. Alternatively, SLA launches but generates zero sales in the first 2 weeks.

### Trigger A (not ready)

April 21 arrives and SLA has no checkout URL, no sales page, and no way to take payment.

### Trigger B (launched but no sales)

SLA launches with a checkout URL and Laura promotes it to her audience. By May 4 (end of Week 4), zero purchases.

### Decision deadline

For Trigger A: April 22. A hard decision must be made: ship an imperfect version this week or build a beta group instead.
For Trigger B: May 4. If zero sales after 2 weeks of promotion, pivot to beta group.

### Ranked actions for Trigger A (not ready to launch)

**Action 1: Launch a beta group of 5 people at a discounted rate**

Instead of a polished product page, Laura DMs 10 warm contacts (people who engaged with content, completed the quiz, or had conversations at networking events) with a personal message:

"Hi [name]. I'm doing a small beta test of my 12-week Strong Lean Athletic program starting next week. I'm looking for 5 people who want to go through it with me, give me feedback, and get a real result. I'm offering it at $99 for beta testers (normally $149). It comes with direct access to me through the program. Would you be interested?"

This approach: generates revenue immediately without a polished product, creates testimonials for the full launch, identifies gaps in the program content before the public launch, and gives Laura 5 committed participants who have real stakes.

5 beta clients at $99 = $495. That funds the full-price launch marketing.

**Action 2: Offer 1-on-1 virtual coaching packages as digital revenue bridge**

If SLA is delayed by more than 2 weeks, expand virtual coaching capacity. Laura currently has Tony and Gina as virtual clients. Adding 2-3 more virtual clients at $115/session (4-pack rate) generates immediate digital revenue without requiring any product packaging.

Promote virtual coaching in content with a different frame than in-person assessment: "If you're not in Key West but want to work with me directly, I have 3 virtual coaching spots opening this month. DM me VIRTUAL." This keeps digital revenue growing while SLA finishes.

**Action 3: Repurpose SLA content as free lead magnets**

If SLA is not ready to sell, pull 2-3 exercises or concepts from the program and post them as free content with the caption: "This is one of the movements from my Strong Lean Athletic program. Full 12-week program drops soon. Drop NOTIFY if you want to be first to know." This builds a waitlist, tests demand, and keeps the program present in Laura's content without requiring a launch.

### Ranked actions for Trigger B (launched but zero sales)

**Action 1: Diagnose the funnel before changing the product**

Zero sales after 2 weeks of promotion usually means one of three things: the audience is too small, the offer framing is wrong, or the sales pathway has friction. Check:

- How many people clicked the Trainerize checkout link? If clicks are low, the problem is the pitch content, not the product.
- How many people completed the Movement Readiness quiz? If the quiz has fewer than 50 completions, the email list is too small to sell a digital product to.
- Is Laura directly promoting SLA in video content, or only mentioning it in captions?

The program cannot sell if no one sees a direct pitch. The Direct Offer script template (Template 7 in the Content Engine) exists for this reason. If it has not been used yet, use it 3 times in the next 7 days.

**Action 2: Move to the beta group offer**

Identical to Action 1 for Trigger A above. A personal DM offer to warm contacts at $99 with direct access to Laura will convert when a passive sales page does not.

**Expected outcome if pivot works**

5 beta clients complete SLA and provide testimonials. Full-price public launch in Month 3 has social proof. Digital revenue in Month 2 comes from virtual coaching expansion ($1,150-$2,300/month from 2-4 new virtual clients). SLA hard launch in Month 3 targets $1,000-$2,000 in first 30 days with proven testimonials.

## What Does Not Change Regardless of Scenarios

These are non-negotiable regardless of which contingencies activate:

1. Price holds at $125 for the Movement Assessment. No discounting except the one-time, time-limited KW launch offer in Scenario 1.
2. Content volume target is 1 post minimum per day. Emergency formats count. No minimum quality standard in Months 1-2.
3. Credential-led positioning stays. Acosta Danza, O-1B, 1,000 performances, psychology degree lead every bio, pitch, and introduction. Non-negotiable.
4. The PT-bridge niche stays. Adults 45+ who need the gap between PT discharge and confident independent training. Do not chase younger demographics because content happens to appeal to them.
5. TikTok-first distribution stays. Repurpose to IG and Facebook. Do not start a new platform.
6. No free coaching as a lead generation tactic. Free pop-up classes (group, 45 min, outdoor) are fine. Free 1-on-1 sessions are not. The beta group at $99 is the floor for individual coaching value.
