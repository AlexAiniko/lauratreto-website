**Tips for delivery:**
- Smile. Make eye contact.
- Pause after "not just harder" — it lands.
- Don't rush the credentials. Let them sink in.

- If they ask a follow-up question, you're in. Listen more than you talk.

### Goals for each visit:

1. **Show up, be helpful, be the best person in the room at what you do.** That's your marketing.
2. **Build rapport with 2-3 people each visit.** Learn their names. Ask about their goals. Remember details for next time.
3. **Drop ONE insight that makes people think "wow, she knows more than most trainers."** Something about hip mobility, scapular stability, breathing mechanics — whatever fits the moment.
4. **When someone says "you're really good"** — that's the opening. Respond naturally:
   - "Thank you! I do private coaching too — if you ever want a deeper look at your movement, I do one-on-one assessments. Here's my card / DM me on Instagram."

---

### DO:

- Be the most knowledgeable person in the room (you ARE)
- Ask people about their injuries, goals, what brought them to Sweat Society
- Offer ONE small adjustment when someone asks for help
- Follow up on Instagram after each visit:
  - "Great meeting you at Sweat Society today! Hope [thing they mentioned] is going well."
- Dress the part. Look like a coach, not a gym-goer.

### DO NOT:

- Hard sell. Ever. You're building relationships, not closing deals.
- Correct people unsolicited (unless they ask or are about to hurt themselves)
- Talk about yourself unprompted. Let them discover your background through natural conversation.
- Pitch to everyone. Be selective — quality connections over quantity.
